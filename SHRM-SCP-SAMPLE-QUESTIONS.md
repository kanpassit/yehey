# SHRM-SCP Sample Questions Preview

Below are representative samples from each of the 4 domains to show the style, difficulty, and quality of the 100-question bank.

## 🔍 ACUITY (Sample 1 of 25)

**Question:** An HR leader identifies that several high-performing employees are leaving the organization within 18 months of promotion. What is the BEST first step to address this retention issue?

**Options:**
- A) Increase compensation for newly promoted employees
- B) Conduct exit interviews and analyze departure patterns
- C) Implement a formal mentoring program for all promoted staff
- D) Create retention bonuses contingent on 2-year tenure

**Correct Answer:** B

**Explanation:** Exit interviews and analysis of departure patterns provide data-driven insights into why employees are leaving, enabling targeted interventions. While mentoring and retention bonuses may help, diagnosis through analysis is the logical first step before implementing solutions.

## 🔍 ACUITY (Sample 2 of 25)

**Question:** A company's healthcare costs have increased 22% year-over-year while industry average is 12%. What is the MOST strategic HR intervention?

**Options:**
- A) Switch to a high-deductible health plan to shift costs to employees
- B) Increase employee cost-sharing for prescription drugs
- C) Conduct a claims analysis and implement targeted wellness programs
- D) Reduce healthcare coverage options to basic plans only

**Correct Answer:** C

**Explanation:** Conducting a claims analysis identifies patterns (obesity, stress-related illness, chronic disease prevalence) enabling targeted wellness interventions. This addresses root causes rather than simply shifting costs or reducing benefits, which can harm employee morale and retention.

## 🎯 STRATEGIC HR MANAGEMENT (Sample 1 of 25)

**Question:** A manufacturing company is automating 40% of production roles over the next three years. Which HR strategic initiative should be prioritized?

**Options:**
- A) Implement layoff procedures for displaced employees
- B) Develop reskilling and career transition programs for affected employees
- C) Accelerate hiring to offset automation losses
- D) Maintain status quo and manage separations as automation progresses

**Correct Answer:** B

**Explanation:** Proactive reskilling programs position the company to retain trained talent, reduce separation costs, preserve morale, and maintain workforce stability during transformation. This is more strategic than reactive layoffs and better than hoping hiring will absorb the changes.

## 🎯 STRATEGIC HR MANAGEMENT (Sample 2 of 25)

**Question:** A healthcare organization's strategic plan emphasizes patient experience and safety. How should HR align talent management to this strategy?

**Options:**
- A) Hire only experienced healthcare workers
- B) Align hiring criteria, training focus, and performance metrics to patient safety and experience outcomes
- C) Increase compensation across all healthcare roles
- D) Implement stricter disciplinary policies

**Correct Answer:** B

**Explanation:** Strategic alignment requires translating business priorities into HR practices. Hiring criteria should assess patient-safety mindset, training should emphasize patient experience, and performance evaluations should reward safety and quality metrics. This creates systemic alignment.

## 🔧 HR EXPERTISE (Sample 1 of 25)

**Question:** An organization's leadership bench is weak for critical roles. What succession planning approach should HR recommend?

**Options:**
- A) Promote the most senior person regardless of readiness
- B) Conduct systematic capability assessment; develop high-potential candidates; create accelerated development paths; establish readiness criteria and timelines
- C) Hire all critical roles externally
- D) Do nothing and address succession when vacancies occur

**Correct Answer:** B

**Explanation:** Robust succession planning requires systematic HR expertise: identifying high-potential talent, assessing capability gaps, designing targeted development, and establishing readiness criteria. This develops internal talent while managing continuity risk.

## 🔧 HR EXPERTISE (Sample 2 of 25)

**Question:** A company wants to implement performance-based pay. What HR expertise is essential to ensure success?

**Options:**
- A) Distribute a portion of base salary based on performance ratings
- B) Design clear performance metrics; establish fair assessment processes; communicate pay-to-performance linkage; train managers on objective evaluation; monitor equity
- C) Implement individual bonuses based on manager discretion
- D) Replace all base salary with performance pay

**Correct Answer:** B

**Explanation:** Performance-based pay requires HR expertise in metric design (clear, measurable, controllable), assessment rigor (training managers, reducing bias), communication (transparency), and monitoring (ensuring equity and effectiveness). Poorly designed systems create demotivation.

## 💼 CONSULTATION & BUSINESS ACUMEN (Sample 1 of 25)

**Question:** A business unit leader asks HR why the customer-facing team has high turnover. What is the BEST consulting question HR should ask first?

**Options:**
- A) Have you considered increasing compensation?
- B) What outcomes are you trying to achieve? What would lower turnover improve—service quality, cost, relationships?
- C) Should we implement retention bonuses?
- D) Have you considered outsourcing customer service?

**Correct Answer:** B

**Explanation:** Effective HR consultation begins with understanding business outcomes, not jumping to solutions. Understanding what the business is trying to achieve clarifies whether turnover is a problem (some turnover may be healthy) or masks other issues. This shows business acumen.

## 💼 CONSULTATION & BUSINESS ACUMEN (Sample 2 of 25)

**Question:** A CEO is frustrated that a new strategic initiative is not progressing despite clear communication. HR is asked to assess what's missing. What is the MOST business-focused approach?

**Options:**
- A) Recommend additional training on the initiative
- B) Assess whether roles and accountability for the initiative are clear; evaluate whether people have resources and capability; identify barriers (competing priorities, unclear consequences, misalignment)
- C) Propose incentives tied to initiative progress
- D) Conduct another communication campaign

**Correct Answer:** B

**Explanation:** Strategic initiative failure usually involves organizational barriers (clarity, resources, priorities, accountability), not communication or motivation alone. HR's business consulting role includes diagnosing execution blockers and proposing systematic changes, not just communication fixes.
